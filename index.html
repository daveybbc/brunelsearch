Pricing

Two engagement models depending on the seniority and complexity of the hire.

Retained Search

Option 1: 20% fee — 1/3 upfront, balance on completion

12-week guarantee on final payment

Option 2: 15% fee — 100% upfront


Why retained:

• Dedicated, priority search with deeper market mapping

• Access to passive and hard-to-reach senior candidates

• More rigorous calibration and shortlisting process

• Better suited for senior, founding, or mission-critical hires

• Faster time-to-signal for complex or ambiguous roles


Retained search is most effective when precision and candidate quality matter more than speed.

Contingency

25% of first-year compensation

8-week replacement guarantee


Why contingency:

• No upfront cost — pay only on successful hire

• Ideal for faster, well-defined hiring needs

• Works well when testing the market quickly

• Suitable for individual or non-urgent hires

• Fully aligned incentives — success-based model


Contingency search is best when speed and flexibility matter more than depth of search.

When to use each model

Use retained search when:

The role is senior, founding-level, or highly specialised (e.g. speech synthesis, source separation, wake word systems), or when hiring precision is critical and the cost of a wrong hire is high.

Use contingency search when:

The role is well-defined, mid-to-senior level, or you want to move quickly and test the market before committing to a deeper search process.
